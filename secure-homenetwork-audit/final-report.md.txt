# 🔐 Home Network Security Audit – Final Report

## 📌 Executive Summary
A security assessment was conducted on a local home network using Kali Linux and Nmap. The objective was to identify active devices, detect exposed services, and evaluate potential security risks.

The scan identified 6 active devices, including a router, personal systems, and IoT devices. The overall network shows a moderate security posture with minimal exposed services.

---

## 🌐 Network Overview

- Network Range: 192.168.x.0/24
- Total Devices Discovered: 6
- Devices include:
  - Router (gateway)
  - Personal computers
  - Smartphone (Xiaomi)
  - IPTV device
  - Unknown devices

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 🔴 High Risk
- Router (192.168.x.1)
  - Exposes HTTP/HTTPS services
  - Critical network control point

- Personal System (192.168.1.8)
  - Remote access service detected earlier (AnyDesk)
  - Potential for unauthorized remote control if misconfigured

---

### 🟡 Medium Risk
- Kali Linux Machine (192.168.1.16)
  - SSH access used for administration
  - Requires proper credential management

---

### 🟢 Low Risk
- Xiaomi Smartphone (192.168.x.9)
- IPTV Device (192.168.x.7)
- Other unknown devices

Reason:
- No open ports detected
- Limited attack surface

---

## 🧠 Risk Assessment Summary

| Risk Level | Count |
|-----------|------|
| High      | 2    |
| Medium    | 1    |
| Low       | 3    |

---

## 🔐 Security Improvements Performed

- Disabled unnecessary remote access services (AnyDesk)
- Verified closure of previously open ports
- Reduced exposed attack surface

---

## ⚠️ Potential Attack Scenarios (Theoretical)

If misconfigured, an attacker could:
- Attempt login on router web interface
- Exploit weak remote access credentials
- Perform network reconnaissance for exposed services

---

## 🛡️ Recommendations

- Use strong passwords for router and systems
- Disable unused services
- Keep devices updated
- Restrict remote access where not required

---

## 📌 Conclusion

The network demonstrates a generally secure configuration with limited exposure. However, critical devices such as the router and personal systems require continuous monitoring and proper configuration to prevent unauthorized access.

---

## ⚠️ Disclaimer

This assessment was conducted on a personal network for educational purposes only. No unauthorized access or exploitation was performed.
